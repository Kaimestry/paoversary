Mommy Euri then finally made some crochet of the Pao, making a random rice bun, adding glasses and horn and some how it looks like Ann. Mommy Euri and Mama Ann both love the crochet so much that they decided to adopt it, naming it Pao. But they didn't know that 'Pao' would also bring someone else along too...

One quiet afternoon, Mama Euri left little Pao to play alone in the warmth of the kitchen. The flames in the hearth danced and crackled, and as Pao peered into them, something strange caught his eye.

There, within the fire was a figure that looked like him. But it wasn't 'him'. This reflection was no pale, innocent Pao; it was a dark, grinning shadow wearing his shape.

“Brother,” the dark Pao called softly from the heart of the fire. “I need your help.” Without a thought, Pao stretched out his hand toward the flames, wanting only to save the poor creature that bore his face. “Thank you, brother,” the shadow murmured as their fingers touched. Neither of them knew it then, but from that single moment, a bond was forged that would outlast eternity.

Later on, the rice Pao would be called "Paok" while his burnt brother is "Paom"
